## Quick Start

### How to Activate Emergency Protocol

**Two Ways:**
1. **Click** the red pulsing emergency button in the video controls
2. **Press** `Ctrl+E` (Windows) or `Cmd+E` (Mac)

---

## When to Use the Emergency Button

Use the emergency button immediately when:
- Client expresses suicidal or homicidal ideation
- Client appears to be in immediate danger
- You need quick access to crisis resources
- Client requires immediate intervention
- You need to contact client's emergency contact

**Note:** This feature is for documentation and resource access. Always follow your practice's clinical emergency protocols.

## Documenting the Emergency

### Required Information:
1. **Emergency Notes** (minimum 10 characters)
   - Describe the situation
   - Actions taken
   - Client's current state
   - Resources used (e.g., "Called 988")
   - Safety plan reviewed
   - Client's agreement to continue

2. **Contact Notification** (checkbox)
   - Check if you notified the emergency contact
   - Leave unchecked if you didn't or if no contact exists

### Example Documentation:
```
Client expressed passive suicidal ideation during session.
Conducted immediate safety assessment - no current plan or
intent. Called emergency contact (spouse) who confirmed
client is safe at home. Reviewed safety plan together.
Client called 988 and spoke with crisis counselor. Client
agreed to continue session and focus on coping strategies.
Will follow up with psychiatrist tomorrow morning.
```

## Resolution Options

### Option 1: Document & Continue Session
- Use when crisis is de-escalated
- Client agrees to safety plan
- Appropriate to continue therapy
- **Effect:** Saves documentation and closes modal, session continues

### Option 2: End Session Immediately
- Use when client needs immediate in-person intervention
- Risk level requires session termination
- Client or clinician cannot safely continue
- **Effect:** Saves documentation, closes modal, AND ends session automatically

### Option 3: Cancel (False Alarm)
- Use if button clicked accidentally
- No emergency actually occurred
- **Effect:** Closes modal, no documentation required

---

## After Emergency Resolution

### If You Continued Session:
1. Session continues normally
2. Emergency documented in database
3. You can review notes after session
4. Follow your practice's post-crisis protocol

### If You Ended Immediately:
1. Session ends automatically
2. Emergency documented in database
3. Client sees "Session Ended" screen
4. Follow up according to your emergency protocol
5. Contact supervisor/clinical director
6. Document in clinical note

## Keyboard Shortcut

**Emergency Activation:** `Ctrl+E` (Windows) or `Cmd+E` (Mac)

**Benefits:**
- Faster than mouse click
- Works even if controls are hidden
- Accessible for keyboard-only users
- Available throughout entire session

**Tips:**
- Practice the shortcut before emergencies
- Works while typing in other fields
- Modal opens immediately

## Troubleshooting

### Emergency Button Not Visible
- **Cause:** You may be logged in as a client
- **Solution:** Emergency button only shows for clinicians

### Can't Click Outside Modal
- **Expected Behavior:** This is intentional
- **Reason:** Forces documentation for compliance
- **Solution:** Choose a resolution option

### No Emergency Contact Displayed
- **Cause:** Client hasn't provided emergency contact
- **Solution:** Continue with standard crisis protocol, update client record later

### Modal Won't Submit
- **Likely Cause:** Documentation too short (< 10 characters)
- **Solution:** Provide more detailed notes

## Practice-Specific Protocols

**Important:** This emergency button is a documentation and resource tool. Always follow your practice's established emergency protocols, which may include:

- Contacting local emergency services (911)
- Notifying your clinical supervisor
- Following mandatory reporting requirements
- Completing incident reports
- Scheduling follow-up appointments
- Coordinating with other providers

**The emergency button does NOT replace your clinical judgment or established protocols.**
